What makes Baduizm a Gem of a Tape

Image from Genius

I was 16, riding home from Braamfontein in a taxi, when a friend of mine Mosa pressed play on “Drama.” In that moment, Erykah Badu hypnotized me, and I’ve been under ever since.

That single track was my initiation into Baduizm, one of the most important records in Neo-Soul. I can only imagine what it must have felt like in 1997 when Erykan Badu dropped her debut, an album that would become a cultural cornerstone. Baduizm is more than R&B: it’s hip-hop grit, soulful depth, subtle jazz, and Badu’s spellbinding voice woven into one timeless masterpiece.

The project is a sonic journey, sometimes upbeat, sometimes mellow, yet maintaining the groove. It captures her youth, her artistry, and her singular worldview. The opener “On & On” sets the tone with its rolling rhythm and mantra-like wisdom: “my cypher keeps moving like a rolling stone.” From there, the mood shifts into “Certainly,” jazzy and effortlessly bouncy, before sinking into the meditative calm of “Next Lifetime” and the storytelling depth of “Otherside of the Game.” Each track feels like another layer of her consciousness.

The industry recognized its brilliance too, Baduizm went triple platinum and earned the 1998 Grammy for Best R&B Album. But its true legacy lives in how it still sounds fresh today, inspiring both artists and listeners nearly three decades later. Whether it’s your first listen or your fiftieth, let Baduizm wash over you again. It’s not just an album, it’s a ritual.
